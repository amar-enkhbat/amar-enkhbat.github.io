Research

Using ML algorithms to recognize actions in human brainwaves

My current research is on "Recognizing an action from EEG brainwave data using Machine Learning (Subject to change)". The basic idea is to recognize certain action from eeg brainwave data using machine learning. For example, we can gather a EEG data from a person who is moving his/her arm, use neural networks to recognize the brainwave associated with the movement then use the generated model to control an robotic prosthetic arm.
My research hasn't really started though. I'm currently studying precursor subjects for my paper i.e introductory computational neuroscience, Bayesian ML and Neural Networks.

Biologically inspired Neural Networks

I'm planning on doing a research on updating methods of neural networks. Lately, backpropagation algorithm's weaknesses are showing. Even Geoffrey Hinton, "the father of the current AI boom", said that he is "deeply suspicious of back-propagation".
The human brain doesn't learn by updating its values of its axons and synapses. Nodes in the human brain are more like individually competent mini robots, kind of like swarm intelligence. My plan is to implement and evaluate neural networks with biologically inspired learning algorithms and compare how they perform to other neural networks with conventional learning rules.
